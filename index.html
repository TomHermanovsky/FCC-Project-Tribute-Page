Writing and Directing

Alan Alda is an active screenwriter and director as well as an actor. During his time on the M*A*S*H series, he wrote and/or directed over 40 episodes. On the big screen he both wrote and directer movies like Sweet Liberty or Four Seasons.
He has also written and published three books (Never Have Your Dog Stuffed, Things I overheard While Talking To Myself and If I Understood You, Would I Have This Look On My Face?).

Other Activities

Alda is famous for his love of science. He hosted the scientific TV series Scientific American Frontiers for 14 years. He's a visiting professor at Stony Brook University and a founder and member of the advisory board of the university's Alan Alda Center for Communicating Science. His podcast Clear+Vivid is focused on communicating as well.
Alda has also done extensive work supporting children hospitals and gender rights, among others.
